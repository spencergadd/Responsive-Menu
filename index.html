Hiking Through the Andes

The Andes are very dry on the Pacific side, but turn to rainforest the further East you travel. These mountains were just starting to get more vegitation.

Travelog

We left from SLC and flew to Guadalajara, MX. It was an open airport with misquitos everywhere. After a 6 hour layover, we flew to Mexico City, where we stayed the night. The next morning we flew to Lima. It was a beautiful city right on the ocean. The beach was black rock. We did some day excursions from Lima for two days and then we flew to Cusco. After a day acclimating to Cusco's altitude, we left on the Inca trail. We hiked for 4 days until we reached Machu Picchu. It was beautiful. After we rode the train back to Cusco, we went to get our Covid-19 tests so we could fly home the next day, but we tested postive! We had to find a private room at our hostel and stick it out until we tested negative.

The Food

Peru is proud of their national dish--the guinea pig! I wasn't brave enough to try it, but it probably tasted like chicken. They did have lots of good food. Our hike was actually catered by a local guide company and they fed us an unbelievable amount of food!

Ruins

All along the hike there were ruins left by the Inca. The trail itself was an amalgamation of stones built by the Inca 500 years ago. Working on the trail was a form of taxation. We also saw ruins outside of Cusco, and on our day excursions from Lima.

Article 2

There are ample places to hike in Utah. We are blessed with immediate access to alpine terrain. But for my wife and I, we also like to travel and see new places. Because of this we have hiked in Nepal, Italy, and all around the Western United States.

We hiked along the Annapurna Circuit in Nepal, along the Meditteranean coast in Cinque Terre, Italy, through Yellowstone, the Sawtooths, and the Windrivers in Wyoming (and Idaho), and we tried to hike Mount Shasta, but it was too hot.

In addition to the exercise aspect of hiking, we also like to include painting, rock climbing, and yoga on our hikes.
